FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about UK vehicle history checks.

How do I search? Do I need a VIN?+

No VIN needed — we use UK registration plates only. Simply enter your full registration number (e.g. LK20 ABC, BD21 SMR). Our system supports all UK plate formats including older-style, personalised, and Northern Ireland plates.

What is an HPI check and do you include it?+

An HPI (Hire Purchase Information) check reveals whether a vehicle has outstanding finance, has been reported stolen, or has been written off as an insurance total loss. Our Standard and Premium reports include full HPI-equivalent data sourced directly from UK lenders and insurers.

What are Cat S and Cat N write-offs?+

Category S means the vehicle has suffered structural damage but has been repaired. Category N means non-structural damage. Both must be declared by sellers. Our reports identify all UK write-off categories (A, B, S, N) sourced from UK insurance databases.

How far back does your MOT history go?+

We retrieve the complete MOT history available from the DVSA database, which typically covers all MOT tests from 2005 onwards. This includes pass/fail status, test dates, mileage at each test, failure reasons, and advisory notices.

Do you offer a refund if my vehicle isn't found?+

Yes, absolutely. If we cannot locate any data for your vehicle within 24 hours of payment, we issue a full refund automatically. We also offer refunds on report quality concerns — contact our UK support team within 48 hours of purchase.

Is my data protected under GDPR?+

Yes. We are fully GDPR compliant, registered with the ICO (Information Commissioner's Office), and process all UK personal data in accordance with UK GDPR. All payments are processed via Stripe (PCI-DSS Level 1) or PayPal. We never sell your data.

How is this different from a DVLA check?+

The DVLA check (gov.uk) only shows basic tax and MOT status. Our reports go far deeper — including full MOT history with advisories, accident records, finance checks, stolen vehicle status, write-off categories, mileage discrepancies, and keeper history. It's a completely different level of detail.
